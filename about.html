About us

Little Angels Public School

Little Angels Public School was founded in the year 1985 with an initial enrollment of 150 students in Gudur town under the auspices of the Paragathi Education Society. Initially offering classes from LKG to Class 6, the school expanded its facilities and offerings over the years. Two years later, the school relocated to Chellakur, where it acquired seven acres of land to accommodate its growing student population, which had increased to 300. Since then, the school has undergone significant development and expansion. Presently, it has a student strength of 1500 students from LKG to 10th grade, with a faculty comprising 75 teachers. The school is affiliated with the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE).

Little Angels Public School has been serving the educational needs of the people of Gudur for over 40 years, offering both residential and day school facilities. It provides excellent hostel accommodations for residential students and ensures convenient transportation and nutritious food for non- resident students. The school boasts a well-stocked library, an audiovisual library, and advanced facilities such as math and English labs to enhance learning outcomes. It emphasizes extracurricular activities and encourages student participation in inter-house, inter-school, and state-level competitions. Discipline and values are instilled in students through various initiatives, and field trips are organized annually for educational enrichment. The Parent-Teacher Association (PTA) plays an active role in the school community, working collaboratively to address concerns, improve facilities, and enhance the overall educational experience for students. Overall, Little Angels Public School is committed to providing a holistic education that fosters academic excellence, character development, and personal growth.
